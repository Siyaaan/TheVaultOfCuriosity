## 1. Narrative Essay

**Purpose:** Tell a story  
**Focus:** Experience, sequence, reflection

### Key Features

- First-person (often)
    
- Chronological structure
    
- Has a lesson or insight
    

### Typical Questions

- “Describe a time when…”
    
- “Tell a story about…”
    

### Example Topic

> A failure that changed how I view success

---

## 2. Descriptive Essay

**Purpose:** Create a vivid picture  
**Focus:** Sensory details (sight, sound, smell, emotion)

### Key Features

- Rich imagery
    
- Figurative language
    
- Less argument, more atmosphere
    

### Typical Questions

- “Describe…”
    
- “Paint a picture of…”
    

### Example Topic

> The feeling of studying alone in the library at night

---

## 3. Expository Essay

**Purpose:** Explain or inform  
**Focus:** Clarity and logic

### Key Features

- Neutral tone
    
- Facts, definitions, examples
    
- No personal opinion
    

### Common Subtypes

- Process (“How something works”)
    
- Cause and effect
    
- Comparison and contrast
    

### Typical Questions

- “Explain how…”
    
- “What is…?”
    

### Example Topic

> How the Zettelkasten method improves learning

---

## 4. Argumentative Essay

**Purpose:** Persuade through reasoning  
**Focus:** Claims, evidence, logic

### Key Features

- Clear thesis
    
- Evidence-based arguments
    
- Counterarguments and rebuttals
    

### Typical Questions

- “Do you agree or disagree?”
    
- “Should…?”
    

### Example Topic

> College should prioritize skill-based learning over exams

---

## 5. Persuasive Essay

**Purpose:** Convince emotionally + logically  
**Focus:** Reader’s beliefs and values

### Difference from Argumentative

- Uses emotion and rhetoric more
    
- Less formal evidence required
    

### Example Topic

> Why students should learn financial literacy early

---

## 6. Analytical Essay

**Purpose:** Break something down and interpret it  
**Focus:** Meaning, patterns, implications

### Key Features

- Close examination
    
- No summary-only writing
    
- Answers “why” and “how”
    

### Typical Subjects

- Literature
    
- Films
    
- Theories
    
- Social issues
    

### Example Topic

> The role of restriction in enhancing creativity

---

## 7. Compare and Contrast Essay

**Purpose:** Examine similarities and differences  
**Focus:** Evaluation and insight

### Structures

- Point-by-point
    
- Block method
    

### Example Topic

> Traditional education vs self-directed learning

---

## 8. Cause and Effect Essay

**Purpose:** Explain why something happens and what results from it

### Example Topic

> The effects of social media on student attention span

---

## 9. Reflective Essay

**Purpose:** Examine personal growth or understanding  
**Focus:** Experience + analysis

### Key Features

- Personal voice
    
- Insight-focused, not storytelling alone
    

### Example Topic

> What learning programming taught me about problem-solving

---

## 10. Research Essay

**Purpose:** Investigate a topic using sources  
**Focus:** Synthesis of evidence

### Key Features

- Multiple academic sources
    
- Citations
    
- Structured argument or explanation
    

### Example Topic

> AI’s impact on accounting education

---

## 11. Critical Essay

**Purpose:** Evaluate and judge  
**Focus:** Strengths, weaknesses, value

### Example Topic

> A critique of standardized testing in higher education

---

## Quick Summary Table

|Type|Main Goal|
|---|---|
|Narrative|Tell a story|
|Descriptive|Create imagery|
|Expository|Explain|
|Argumentative|Prove a claim|
|Persuasive|Convince|
|Analytical|Interpret|
|Compare & Contrast|Evaluate differences|
|Cause & Effect|Explain consequences|
|Reflective|Show learning|
|Research|Synthesize sources|
|Critical|Judge quality/value|
