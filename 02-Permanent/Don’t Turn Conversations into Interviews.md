Good conversations require balance. Constantly asking someone about their life turns interaction into an interview rather than a shared exchange. Once you understand a person’s interests or desires, abstract them into themes and speak from those ideas instead of repeatedly questioning them. This shifts the conversation from extraction to contribution.
